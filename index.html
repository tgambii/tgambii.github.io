About me

Engineer, problem solver, builder.

I'm a mechanical design engineer based in Boston, MA focused on turning complex engineering challenges into clean, manufacturable solutions.

Currently at Curtiss-Wright in Tewksbury, working on defence-grade mechanical systems — from redesigning mobile ground stations for military radios to managing CAD data infrastructure and SAP BOMs. My earlier internships at Parker Hannifin and ACAD Design gave me a broad foundation across nitrogen systems, vision systems, and precision fixtures.

Outside of engineering I'm a competitive ice hockey player — five years of Division I Club Hockey at RIT, including two as team treasurer and a season as a practice player for the Women's D1 NCAA team.

Hands-on first. I prototype early and often — physical builds surface problems no simulation will catch.
Systems thinking. Good design considers manufacturing, assembly, and the end user simultaneously.
Clear documentation. The best design is worthless without thorough technical writing behind it.
