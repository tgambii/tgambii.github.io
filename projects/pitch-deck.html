01 — Problem
The problem
Locker rooms are biologically hostile environments.
Post-game conditions routinely exceed safe thresholds for humidity, air quality, and pathogen load.
💧
Extreme humidity
Post-game locker rooms hit 80–95% RH. 40+ athletes generate 3–6 pints of moisture per hour — mold and bacteria grow within hours.
☣️
Ammonia and VOC off-gassing
Sweat decomposition releases ammonia and organic acids. Standard HVAC filters don't neutralise these — KMnO₄ carbon is required.
🦠
Airborne pathogens
Athlete's foot, ringworm, staph, and MRSA spread via airborne spores. Known occupational hazards in professional locker rooms.
🚌
Travel kills fixed solutions
Teams spend 40–80 nights away per season. Permanent HVAC only protects home venues — visiting facilities are unaddressed.

Why desiccant over compressor
Silent. Effective 40°F–100°F. No refrigerant. 10,000+ hour rotor lifespan (8–12 years). Compressor units run at 62–68 dB with refrigerant shipping restrictions — non-starters for travel.
Why no water reservoir
3–6 pints/hour would require a reservoir adding 6–12 lb that needs manual emptying every session. Desiccant exhaust uses a 4" flex hose to any drain or window. Continuous and hands-free.

Airflow: Room air → Grilles (F) → Pre-mesh → HEPA H13 → Carbon/KMnO₄ → UV-C → Desiccant rotor (D) → Blower (C) → Clean dry air upward (A). Separate regen airstream exhausts moisture via rear port (G). The two airstreams never mix.

Fan sizing
800 ft² × 10 ft = 8,000 ft³ · 2–3 ACH = 267–400 CFM · Target: 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded · Forward-curved centrifugal blower + EC variable-speed motor
Six non-negotiable constraints
→ Fan curve — 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c.
→ Filter face area — 14"×16" min, 200 FPM max
→ Carbon bed — 1.5–2" for ammonia dwell
→ Rotor velocity — 150–250 FPM
→ ΔP sensing — real-time, not a timer
→ Motor cooling — in-airstream + thermal cutoff

Why desiccant is quieter
Compressors have three noise sources: piston, condenser fan, evaporator fan. Zefyr Sport has one: the centrifugal blower. The rotor drive runs at very low RPM and is effectively inaudible. The regen heater is completely silent.
