01 — Problem
The problem
Locker rooms are biologically hostile environments.
Post-game conditions routinely exceed safe thresholds for humidity, air quality, and pathogen load.
💧
Extreme humidity
Post-game locker rooms regularly hit 80–95% relative humidity. Wet gear, post-shower steam, and 40+ athletes in an enclosed space create conditions that promote mold and bacterial growth within hours.
☣️
Ammonia and VOC off-gassing
Sweat decomposition releases ammonia, sulfur compounds, and volatile organic acids. Standard HVAC filters don't neutralize these — they only filter particulates.
🦠
Fungal and bacterial spores
Athlete's foot, ringworm, staph, and MRSA spread via airborne spore vectors in high-humidity environments. Known occupational hazards in professional locker rooms.
🚌
Travel kills any fixed solution
Teams spend 40–80 nights per season in away facilities. Permanent HVAC upgrades only protect home venues — visiting locker rooms and tournament sites are unaddressed.

Why no water reservoir?
A post-game locker room generates 3–6 pints of moisture per hour. A reservoir large enough for a 90-minute session would add 6–12 lb and require manual emptying after every use. AirLock Pro uses a desiccant exhaust system: moisture is expelled as warm humid air through a 4" flex hose to any floor drain or window. Continuous, hands-free, zero tank weight.
Why desiccant over compressor?
Compressor units are heavy, loud (62–68 dB), and struggle at cooler temps. Desiccant rotors are silent, lightweight, effective from 40°F–100°F, require no refrigerant, and have a rated lifespan of 10,000+ hours — eliminating regulatory and shipping constraints for air travel.

Fan sizing math
Room: 800 ft² × 10 ft ceiling = 8,000 ft³
Target: 2–3 air changes/hr = 267–400 CFM
Design target: 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c.
Fan type: Forward-curved centrifugal blower — maintains CFM at high static pressure unlike axial fans which fall off the curve sharply
Filter face velocity constraint
Max face velocity: 200 FPM (exceed this and particulates bypass HEPA media)
Required face area: 300 CFM ÷ 200 FPM = 1.5 ft² minimum
Filter dimensions: 14" × 16" — non-negotiable chassis constraint
Dirty filter problem — 3 solutions
EC variable speed motor — ramps up as resistance rises, maintains 300 CFM. Best solution. +$18–25 COGS.
Differential pressure sensor — real-time filter load detection. Triggers LED alert. ~$4 BOM cost.
Oversized filter media — 20–25% larger than minimum calc. Extends service life, flattens performance cliff.
Six non-negotiable sizing constraints
1. Fan curve — centrifugal, 300 CFM at 1.5+ in. w.c. loaded
2. Filter face area — 14"×16" min, 200 FPM max velocity
3. Carbon bed depth — 1.5–2" min for ammonia dwell time
4. Rotor face velocity — 150–250 FPM, 10–12" diameter rotor
5. Pressure sensing — differential sensor for live filter load
6. Motor cooling — positioned in airstream, thermal cutoff backup

Why desiccant is quieter
Compressor dehumidifiers have three noise sources: compressor piston, condenser fan, and evaporator fan. AirLock Pro has one: the centrifugal blower. The rotor drive motor runs at very low RPM and is effectively inaudible. The regeneration heater is completely silent.
The key differentiator
