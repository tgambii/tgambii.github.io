The problem

Locker rooms are biologically hostile environments.

Post-game conditions routinely exceed safe thresholds across humidity, air quality, and pathogen load — simultaneously.

💧 Extreme humidity

Post-game locker rooms regularly reach 80–95% RH. A session with 40 athletes generates 3–6 pints of moisture per hour. Mold and bacterial growth can begin within hours.

☣️ Ammonia and VOC off-gassing

Sweat decomposition releases ammonia and volatile organic acids. Standard HVAC offers no chemical neutralisation. KMnO₄-impregnated carbon is required — absent from virtually all consumer purifiers.

🦠 Airborne pathogens

Athlete's foot, ringworm, staph, and MRSA spread via airborne spores in high-humidity environments. Known occupational hazards with documented liability implications for teams.

🚌 The travel gap

Teams spend 40–80 nights per season in away facilities. Permanent HVAC only protects home venues. Visiting locker rooms and tournament sites are completely unaddressed.

Key insight: No product on the market combines dehumidification, HEPA filtration, and ammonia neutralisation in a portable, travel-ready form factor designed for athletic use.

Why desiccant over compressor

Silent operation. Effective 40°F–100°F. No refrigerant. 10,000+ hour rotor lifespan (8–12 years at typical use). Compressor units run at 62–68 dB with refrigerant restrictions — two non-starters for travel.

Why no water reservoir

3–6 pints/hour means a sufficient reservoir adds 6–12 lb and requires manual emptying every session. Desiccant exhaust expels moisture continuously through a 4" flex hose. Continuous, hands-free, zero weight penalty.

Fan must cover loaded case

Fan sizing: 800 ft² × 10 ft = 8,000 ft³ · 2–3 ACH = 267–400 CFM · Design target: 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded · Forward-curved centrifugal blower with variable-speed EC motor.

Six non-negotiable constraints

  • Fan curve — 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded
  • Filter face area — 14"×16" min, 200 FPM max
  • Carbon bed depth — 1.5–2" min for ammonia dwell
  • Rotor face velocity — 150–250 FPM, 10–12" dia.
  • ΔP sensing — real-time, not a timer
  • Motor cooling — in-airstream + thermal cutoff

Dirty-filter solutions

  • EC variable speed motor — ramps as resistance rises. +$18–25 COGS.
  • ΔP sensor — actual load measurement. ~$4 BOM.
  • Oversized media — 20–25% larger than minimum calc.

Filter replacement UX

Under 60 seconds. No tools. Zero confusion.

Physical access design

  • Quarter-turn latch — one thumb twist, door drops open as a shelf.
  • Rubber perimeter gasket — prevents air bypassing the filter stack.
  • Reed switch interlock — cuts fan power on door open. Restores on close.
  • Inner door diagram — numbered 1→2→3 silkscreened on door face.

Cartridge design

  • Colour-coded tabs — gray, blue, black. Identifiable before removal.
  • Asymmetric keying — physically impossible to install backwards.
  • Click-to-seat detent — audible and tactile confirmation of seating.
  • Foil-sealed carbon cartridge — activates on opening. Ships inert.

Replacement sequence

  1. Unit stays plugged in. Reed switch cuts fan on door open.
  2. Quarter-turn latch. Door drops open as shelf.
  3. Pull colour-coded tabs. Cartridges slide straight out.
  4. Insert new cartridges tab-first. Click confirms seating.
  5. Close door, quarter-turn. Fan resumes automatically.
  6. Tap "Filter replaced" in app to reset ΔP baseline.
