The problem

Locker rooms are biologically hostile environments.

Post-game conditions routinely exceed safe thresholds across humidity, air quality, and pathogen load simultaneously — a combination no existing product addresses.

💧

Extreme humidity

Post-game locker rooms regularly reach 80–95% RH. A session with 40 athletes, wet gear, and post-shower steam generates 3–6 pints of moisture per hour. Mold and bacterial growth can begin within hours.

☣️

Ammonia and VOC off-gassing

Sweat decomposition releases ammonia and volatile organic acids. Standard HVAC filters offer no chemical neutralisation. KMnO₄-impregnated carbon is required — absent from virtually all consumer purifiers.

🦠

Airborne pathogens

Athlete's foot, ringworm, staph, and MRSA spread via airborne spores in high-humidity environments. Known occupational hazards with documented liability implications for teams.

🚌

The travel gap

Teams spend 40–80 nights per season in away facilities. Permanent HVAC only protects home venues. Visiting locker rooms, tournament sites, and hotel gyms are completely unaddressed.

Key insight: No product on the market combines dehumidification, HEPA filtration, and ammonia neutralisation in a portable, travel-ready form factor designed for athletic use.

Why desiccant over compressor

Silent operation. Effective 40°F–100°F. No refrigerant. 10,000+ hour rotor lifespan (8–12 years at typical team use). Compressor units run at 62–68 dB and carry refrigerant shipping restrictions — two non-starters for travel-based use.

Why no water reservoir

3–6 pints/hour of moisture generation means a sufficient reservoir adds 6–12 lb and requires manual emptying after every session. Desiccant exhaust expels moisture continuously through a 4" flex hose to any floor drain or window. Continuous, hands-free, zero weight penalty.

Sub-assembly architecture

Fan must cover loaded case

Fan sizing: 800 ft² × 10 ft = 8,000 ft³ · 2–3 ACH = 267–400 CFM · Design target: 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded · Fan type: forward-curved centrifugal blower with variable-speed EC motor.

Six non-negotiable constraints

  • Fan curve — centrifugal blower, 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded
  • Filter face area — 14"×16" min, 200 FPM max face velocity
  • Carbon bed depth — 1.5–2" min for ammonia dwell time
  • Rotor face velocity — 150–250 FPM, 10–12" diameter
  • ΔP sensing — real-time filter load, not a timer
  • Motor cooling — in-airstream positioning + thermal cutoff

Dirty-filter solutions

  • EC variable speed motor — ramps fan speed as resistance rises, maintains 300 CFM. Best solution. Adds ~$18–25 COGS.
  • ΔP sensor — actual loading measurement drives alert. ~$4 BOM cost.
  • Oversized media — 20–25% larger than minimum extends intervals and flattens the degradation curve.

Filter replacement UX

Under 60 seconds. No tools. Zero confusion.

If replacement feels like a chore, filters don't get changed on time, performance degrades, and the product gets blamed for a maintenance failure.

Physical access design

  • Quarter-turn latch — one thumb twist, door drops open. No screws, no clips to lose.
  • Bottom-hinged door — acts as a shelf during the swap. No juggling while pulling filters.
  • Rubber perimeter gasket — prevents air bypassing the filter stack.
  • Reed switch interlock — cuts fan power when door opens. Restores automatically on close.
  • Inner door diagram — numbered 1→2→3 installation guide silkscreened on door face.

Cartridge design

  • Colour-coded tabs — pre-filter gray, HEPA blue, carbon black.
  • Asymmetric keying — physically impossible to install backwards.
  • Click-to-seat detent — audible and tactile confirmation of correct seating.
  • Printed expiry label on tab face — analog backup for non-app users.
  • Foil-sealed carbon cartridge — activates on opening. Ships inert.

The problem

Locker rooms are biologically hostile environments.

Post-game conditions routinely exceed safe thresholds across humidity, air quality, and pathogen load simultaneously — a combination no existing product addresses.

💧

Extreme humidity

Post-game locker rooms regularly reach 80–95% RH. A session with 40 athletes, wet gear, and post-shower steam generates 3–6 pints of moisture per hour. Mold and bacterial growth can begin within hours.

☣️

Ammonia and VOC off-gassing

Sweat decomposition releases ammonia and volatile organic acids. Standard HVAC filters offer no chemical neutralisation. KMnO₄-impregnated carbon is required — absent from virtually all consumer purifiers.

🦠

Airborne pathogens

Athlete's foot, ringworm, staph, and MRSA spread via airborne spores in high-humidity environments. Known occupational hazards with documented liability implications for teams.

🚌

The travel gap

Teams spend 40–80 nights per season in away facilities. Permanent HVAC only protects home venues. Visiting locker rooms, tournament sites, and hotel gyms are completely unaddressed.

Key insight: No product on the market combines dehumidification, HEPA filtration, and ammonia neutralisation in a portable, travel-ready form factor designed for athletic use.

Why desiccant over compressor

Silent operation. Effective 40°F–100°F. No refrigerant. 10,000+ hour rotor lifespan (8–12 years at typical team use). Compressor units run at 62–68 dB and carry refrigerant shipping restrictions — two non-starters for travel-based use.

Why no water reservoir

3–6 pints/hour of moisture generation means a sufficient reservoir adds 6–12 lb and requires manual emptying after every session. Desiccant exhaust expels moisture continuously through a 4" flex hose to any floor drain or window. Continuous, hands-free, zero weight penalty.

Fan must cover loaded case

Fan sizing: 800 ft² × 10 ft = 8,000 ft³ · 2–3 ACH = 267–400 CFM · Design target: 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded · Fan type: forward-curved centrifugal blower with variable-speed EC motor.

Six non-negotiable constraints

  • Fan curve — centrifugal blower, 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded
  • Filter face area — 14"×16" min, 200 FPM max face velocity
  • Carbon bed depth — 1.5–2" min for ammonia dwell time
  • Rotor face velocity — 150–250 FPM, 10–12" diameter
  • ΔP sensing — real-time filter load, not a timer
  • Motor cooling — in-airstream positioning + thermal cutoff

Dirty-filter solutions

  • EC variable speed motor — ramps fan speed as resistance rises, maintains 300 CFM. Best solution. Adds ~$18–25 COGS.
  • ΔP sensor — actual loading measurement drives alert. ~$4 BOM cost.
  • Oversized media — 20–25% larger than minimum extends intervals and flattens the degradation curve.

Filter replacement UX

Under 60 seconds. No tools. Zero confusion.

If replacement feels like a chore, filters don't get changed on time, performance degrades, and the product gets blamed for a maintenance failure.

Physical access design

  • Quarter-turn latch — one thumb twist, door drops open. No screws, no clips to lose.
  • Bottom-hinged door — acts as a shelf during the swap. No juggling while pulling filters.
  • Rubber perimeter gasket — prevents air bypassing the filter stack.
  • Reed switch interlock — cuts fan power when door opens. Restores automatically on close.
  • Inner door diagram — numbered 1→2→3 installation guide silkscreened on door face.

Cartridge design

  • Colour-coded tabs — pre-filter gray, HEPA blue, carbon black.
  • Asymmetric keying — physically impossible to install backwards.
  • Click-to-seat detent — audible and tactile confirmation of correct seating.
  • Printed expiry label on tab face — analog backup for non-app users.
  • Foil-sealed carbon cartridge — activates on opening. Ships inert.
