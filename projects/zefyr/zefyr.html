Pitch deck

The full product breakdown.

The problem

Locker rooms are biologically hostile environments.

Extreme humidity

Post-game locker rooms regularly reach 80–95% RH. A session with 40 athletes generates 3–6 pints of moisture per hour. Mold and bacterial growth can begin within hours.

Ammonia and VOC off-gassing

Sweat decomposition releases ammonia and volatile organic acids. Standard HVAC offers no chemical neutralisation. KMnO₄-impregnated carbon is required — absent from virtually all consumer purifiers.

Airborne pathogens

Athlete's foot, ringworm, staph, and MRSA spread via airborne spores in high-humidity environments — documented occupational hazards with liability implications.

The travel gap

Teams spend 40–80 nights per season away. Permanent HVAC only protects home venues. Visiting locker rooms and tournament sites are completely unaddressed.

Key insight: No product combines dehumidification, HEPA filtration, and ammonia neutralisation in a portable, travel-ready form factor designed for athletic use.

Why desiccant over compressor

Silent operation. Effective 40°F–100°F. No refrigerant. 10,000+ hour rotor lifespan (8–12 years typical). Compressor units run at 62–68 dB and carry refrigerant restrictions — non-starters for travel.

Why no water reservoir

3–6 pints/hour means a sufficient reservoir adds 6–12 lb and needs emptying every session. Desiccant exhaust expels moisture through a 4" flex hose to any floor drain or window. Continuous, hands-free.

Fan must cover loaded case

Fan sizing: 800 ft² × 10 ft ceiling = 8,000 ft³ · 2–3 ACH = 267–400 CFM · Design target: 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded · Forward-curved centrifugal blower with variable-speed EC motor.

Six non-negotiable constraints

  • → Fan curve — 300 CFM at ≥1.5 in. w.c. loaded
  • → Filter face area — 14"×16" min, 200 FPM max
  • → Carbon bed depth — 1.5–2" for ammonia dwell
  • → Rotor face velocity — 150–250 FPM, 10–12" dia.
  • → ΔP sensing — real-time, not a timer
  • → Motor cooling — in-airstream + thermal cutoff

Dirty-filter solutions

  • → EC variable speed motor — ramps as resistance rises. +$18–25 COGS.
  • → ΔP sensor — actual load measurement. ~$4 BOM.
  • → Oversized media — 20–25% larger than minimum calc. Flattens performance cliff.
